The Welte-Mignon Has a Faculty of One Hundred and Fifty-Six Instructors in Piano Playing The Welte-Mignon can be likened to a college for teaching the art of playing the piano. This college has a faculty of one hundred fifty-six instructors-the leading pianists of this generation. These master pianists have interpreted over five thousand different pieces of the best musical literature of all ages of which each inter- pretation is an authoritative instruction in the playing off that particular composition -a final lesson in the art of finished piano playing. playing. The Welte-Mignon is the means through which this great storehouse of cor- rect and artistic piano playing is given to the public-the student and layman, alike. All of the great musical conservatories of the world, if taken together, could not com- mand a faculty of such combined power and genius as the faculty of piano inter- pretation employed by the Welte- Mignon. Nor can all the combined musical schools find it within their means to render the great masterpieces of musical literature with such authority as can the Welte Mignon.
